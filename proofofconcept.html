Perception

Proof of Concept for LUMA 2026

Concept

I plan to create a series of long fabric panels dyed with hikizome techniques, using gradients of indigo and other colors. The dyed cloth will then be used as a surface for projection mapping, layering projected light onto physical color. The work is an exploration of what color actually is. Dye and light are both experienced as color, yet they exist in fundamentally different ways: one through pigment absorbed into a material, the other through wavelengths of projected light. By combining them, I want to blur the boundary between the two and create moments where it becomes unclear whether a color belongs to the cloth, the projection, or neither. The project brings together traditional dyeing techniques and digital media as a way to investigate color as both a physical and perceptual phenomenon.

Prototype

The following is a prototype of the final project. At first glance, it is unclear which of the two fabrics is dyed. By placing a paper in front of each panel, we can see that the left panel is dyed with white light projected on while the right panel is white fabric with a gradient projected on.
